French Toast

Golden, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, French toast is a classic breakfast favorite that never goes out of style. Made by dipping thick slices of bread in a rich egg and milk mixture, then pan-frying them to perfection, this dish is both comforting and indulgent. Whether topped with a dusting of powdered sugar, a drizzle of maple syrup, or a handful of fresh berries, French toast offers the perfect balance of sweetness and warmth. Ideal for weekend brunches or a cozy morning treat, this easy-to-make recipe is sure to become a go-to in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • Bread
  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Milk

Have you prepared all the ingredients? Alright, let's get to cooking

  1. In a shallow bowl, crack the eggs and whisk until smooth.
  2. Add milk and a pinch of salt.
  3. Whisk everything until well combined.
  4. Heat a skillet or a nonstick pan over medium heat.
  5. Add a small amount of butter to coat the surface.
  6. Dip a slice of bread into the egg mixture.
  7. Let it soak for about 10-20 seconds per side, depending on the thickness and staleness of the bread.
  8. Make sure both sides are coated.
  9. Place the soaked bread onto the hot skiller or pan
  10. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side or until golden brown.
  11. Flip carefully with a spatula.
  12. Remove from pan and serve immediately while warm.
  13. Add your favorite toppings and it is ready to be served.
